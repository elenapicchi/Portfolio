Context

The opportunity

Vonder operates premium co-living spaces across London, providing fully furnished apartments and office spaces to a community of young professionals. Every apartment comes with a welcome kit and ongoing supplies — a touchpoint that, until this project, had no sustainability criteria.

I identified an opportunity to use Vonder's purchasing power to replace conventional household and cleaning products with sustainable alternatives — making eco-conscious choices effortless for residents rather than optional.

Approach

How I evaluated and selected partners

Rather than approaching this as a marketing exercise, I treated it as a values-driven procurement decision. Each potential partner was evaluated against a set of criteria before any conversation about commercial terms.

🌿

Environmental credentials

Plastic-free packaging, sustainable sourcing, carbon footprint reduction — verified, not just claimed

✅

Product quality

Sustainable alternatives needed to match or exceed conventional products — resident experience could not be compromised

🤝

Mission alignment

Partners needed to share a genuine commitment to reducing environmental impact, not just use sustainability as a marketing angle

📦

Operational fit

Products needed to work at Vonder's scale — reliable supply, consistent packaging, easy integration into the welcome kit workflow

Partners

Why these three

Homethings and Smol were selected as complementary welcome kit inclusions for apartments. Both eliminate single-use plastic from household cleaning entirely — Homethings through compostable concentrated formats, Smol through dissolvable tablets. Together they covered the full range of cleaning needs without a single plastic bottle.

Cheeky Panda replaced conventional toilet paper across both apartments and office spaces. Made from bamboo — a rapidly renewable resource — and packaged without plastic, it offered a straightforward, high-impact substitution with no change to the resident experience.

Process

From identification to activation

  • Researched and mapped the sustainable lifestyle brand landscape, identifying brands with verified environmental credentials rather than greenwashing
  • Reached out to prospective partners with a clear value proposition — access to Vonder's community of environmentally conscious urban professionals
  • Negotiated partnership terms that worked commercially for Vonder while supporting the partner brands' growth and mission
  • Managed the integration of partner products into the welcome kit workflow, coordinating with operations to ensure seamless delivery
  • Built ongoing relationships with brand contacts to manage supply, gather feedback and explore future collaboration

Outcomes

What this achieved

  • Three sustainable brand partnerships activated and integrated into Vonder's operational supply chain
  • Single-use plastic cleaning products eliminated from the welcome kit across all apartments
  • Conventional toilet paper replaced with bamboo alternative across offices and apartments
  • Vonder residents given access to sustainable alternatives as a default — removing the friction of making eco-conscious choices individually
  • 40%+ increase in brand engagement through community campaigns connected to the sustainability partnerships

Reflection

What this taught me

This project showed me that sustainability change does not always require grand gestures — sometimes it means identifying the right leverage point and making the better choice the easy choice. By changing what Vonder bought and put in front of residents by default, we removed a barrier that most people never think to climb.

It also reinforced how important rigorous partner evaluation is. Several brands approached us with strong marketing but weak credentials. Learning to distinguish genuine environmental commitment from positioning was a skill I carried directly into my work at Ruby Cup — and one I want to develop further in grant programme work, where assessing organisational integrity and impact potential is central to the role.
